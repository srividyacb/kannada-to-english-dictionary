About Reverend Ferdinand Kittel

 

Reverend Ferdinand Kittel (Kannada: ಫರ್ಡಿನ್ಯಾಂಡ್ ಕಿಟ್ಟೆಲ್) (7 April 1832 in Resterhafe, East Frisia – 18 December 1903 in Tubingen) was a priest and indologist with the Basel Mission in south India and worked in Mangalore, Madikeri and Dharawad in Karnataka. He is most famous for his studies of the Kannada language and for producing the first ever Kannada-English dictionary of about 70,000 words in 1894. He also composed numerous Kannada poems.

He arrived in India in 1853. As a missionary, he endeavored to follow Paul's first Epistle to the Corianthians and "become as an Indian unto the Indians", and undertook exhaustive studies learning the Kannada language, customs and local music.

Reverend Ferdinand Kittel also wrote a book on Kannada Grammar called A Grammar of the Kannada Language: Comprising the Three Dialects of the language. He translated Nagavarma's work on Kannada prosody.

He is credited with shaping the Kannada language and the regional identity of Kannada speakers. Many educational institutions have been named after him.
